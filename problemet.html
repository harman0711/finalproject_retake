CLOTHING WASTE TODAY

In today's society we throw away more clothes than ever before. When you throw away clothes in the trash, it is not only the clothes that go to waste. The water, the material and the natural resources that were used are discarded. The contributions of the workers is wasted. Even the fuel used to carry the clothes around is wasted.


In this way, current production and consumption are not sustainable. The number of clothes thrown away is 92 million tonnes of textile waste per year. According to researchers, this number will continue to increase to as much as 137 million by 2030 unless we do something.

SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPEMENT GOALS

We at Genesis have a goal of being able to work towards the UN's 12th sustainability goal "Responsible consumption and production", by focusing on the sub-goal 12.5 (By 2030, reduce the amount of waste significantly through prevention, reduction, material recycling and reuse).


At the same time, our efforts to reduce over-consumption of clothing will lead to a greener future, reduce unnecessary textile production and reduce climate change. Therefore, we are also partly working towards the UN's 13th sustainability goal "Climate action".

Why we believe that Genesis can make an impact

In this day and age climate change is a well spoken about term. But how can a regular person like you and me make an impact. You might try to take the bus instead of your car, but some might not have that choice, because they might live in a rural community. That’s the reason why we chose to focus on clothing. Everybody buys and need clothes, every single day. That’s why we wanted to create something that is inclusive, relatable and informational for everyone.


This topic is very little spoken about and people doesn’t know about the damage the clothing industry causes on our planet and people. And that’s why we created Genesis to make people aware of your next purchases or the products you already own. To make better decision’s in the future and be aware of what clothing industry causes in damages.
